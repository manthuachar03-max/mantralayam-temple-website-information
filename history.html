History of Sri Raghavendra Swamy Mutt

Sri Raghavendra Swamy Mutt is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage center located in Mantralayam, Andhra Pradesh, on the peaceful banks of the Tungabhadra River. It is one of the most respected spiritual destinations in India and attracts millions of devotees every year.

The Mutt belongs to the ancient Guru Parampara established by Sri Madhwacharya, the founder of the Dvaita Vedanta philosophy. For centuries, the Mutt has preserved Vedic traditions, promoted spiritual knowledge, and guided devotees through the teachings of great saints.

Sri Raghavendra Swamy, born as Venkatanatha in 1595, became one of the greatest scholars and spiritual leaders of the Dvaita tradition. His wisdom, compassion, miracles, and unwavering devotion to Lord Vishnu inspired countless devotees across India.

In the year 1671, Sri Raghavendra Swamy entered the sacred Jeeva Brindavana at Mantralayam while still alive, promising to bless and protect sincere devotees for generations. His Brindavana continues to be the spiritual heart of the Mutt and a symbol of eternal faith.

Every day, thousands of pilgrims visit the Mutt to participate in darshan, sevas, Vedic chanting, and Annadanam (free meals). Major festivals such as Sri Raghavendra Swamy Aradhana attract lakhs of devotees from different parts of the country.

Today, Sri Raghavendra Swamy Mutt stands as a beacon of devotion, peace, and spiritual enlightenment. It continues to preserve ancient traditions while welcoming devotees from around the world who seek blessings, inner peace, and divine guidance.
