About Sri Raghavendra Swamy Mutt

Sri Raghavendra Swamy Mutt, located in Mantralayam on the sacred banks of the Tungabhadra River in Andhra Pradesh, is one of the most revered pilgrimage centers in India.

The Mutt belongs to the unbroken Guru Parampara established by the great philosopher Sri Madhwacharya, the founder of the Dvaita Vedanta philosophy.

The Mutt became world-famous through the divine life and teachings of Sri Raghavendra Swamy, a saint, scholar, philosopher, and spiritual guide who dedicated his life to spreading devotion, knowledge, and compassion.

In 1671, Sri Raghavendra Swamy entered Jeeva Brindavana (a state of eternal meditation while alive) at Mantralayam, blessing devotees with the promise that he would continue to protect and guide devotees for centuries.

Today, millions of devotees from across India and around the world visit Mantralayam every year to seek the blessings of Sri Raghavendra Swamy. The sacred Brindavana stands as a symbol of faith, hope, miracles, and spiritual peace.
